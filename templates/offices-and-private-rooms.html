Offices and Private Rooms

Listen to Tour Audio:

The Mepkin Abbey Monastery in Moncks Corner, South Carolina, designed to support the monastic life of Trappist monks, includes various offices and private rooms integral to their community and operations. These spaces are typically not open to the public, as they are part of the private areas where the monks live and work. The monastery’s layout is structured to accommodate both the spiritual and practical needs of the monastic community, including areas for prayer, study, and administration.

1. Offices: These are likely used for the administrative functions necessary to manage the monastery, its various programs, and its interactions with the outside world.
2. Private Rooms (Cells): In monastic terms, a monk’s private room is often referred to as a “cell”. These cells are simple living quarters where monks spend time in prayer, meditation, and personal reflection, away from the communal areas of the monastery.

Mepkin Abbey, following the Rule of St. Benedict, places a strong emphasis on the values of prayer, work, study, and community life. The design and use of their spaces reflect this, balancing communal activities with the need for solitude and contemplation. Visitors to Mepkin Abbey are usually restricted to public areas and are offered guided tours to learn more about the monastery and its way of life.
